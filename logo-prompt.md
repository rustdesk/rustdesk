# Task: Design a logo for Tabby

You are designing the official app icon and logo for **Tabby**, an iOS app that gives remote desktop control "extra paws" — a power-user RustDesk client with a custom keyboard overlay, gestures, and macros.

## Brand essence

- **Name:** Tabby
- **Tagline:** "Tabby gives your remote desktop extra paws." 🐾
- **Double meaning of "paws":** literal extra digits/keys (Tabs, arrows, modifiers) AND a homophone for "powers" (sticky modifiers, macros, shortcuts, gestures — extra power-user capabilities)
- **Audience:** Senior developers, power users, people who care about mechanical keyboards and terminal aesthetics. Not a consumer app. Adjacent to: Kitty terminal, Catppuccin, Ghostty, Raycast, Linear.
- **Personality:** Confident, playful, slightly mischievous — but precise and technical. Not cute. Not corporate. Think "the cat that knows exactly what it's doing."


### Anti-patterns (DO NOT)

- ❌ Cartoon cat with big eyes
- ❌ Realistic photographic cat
- ❌ Pawprint with hearts, stars, or sparkles
- ❌ Comic Sans or rounded "fun" fonts
- ❌ Rainbow gradients or maximalist color
- ❌ 3D rendering or skeuomorphic depth
- ❌ Anything that looks like a pet-shop or veterinary logo
- ❌ Anything that looks like an existing big-tech logo (no Apple-paw, no Google-cat)

## Deliverables

Generate the following set:

1. **Primary app icon** — 1024×1024px, square, ready for App Store submission. Should work on both light and dark iOS home screens (iOS 18+ supports dark/tinted variants — bonus if you provide a dark variant).

2. **Wordmark** — "Tabby" in a clean, modern sans-serif (suggestions: Geist, Inter, IBM Plex Sans, or designer's choice). The letter "a" or "y" can have a subtle nod to the cat metaphor (e.g., a whisker stroke, a slit-eye dot) IF it doesn't compromise legibility.

3. **Lockup (mark + wordmark)** — horizontal arrangement, mark on the left, wordmark on the right. For use in README, GitHub, marketing.

4. **Monochrome variants** — pure black on transparent, pure white on transparent. For favicons, terminal output, single-color contexts.

5. **Three concepts before final.** Show three distinct directions before refining one. Don't converge too early.

## Process

1. Sketch three distinct concepts (paw, eye, silhouette — or three paw variations, etc.). Show small thumbnails first.
2. Wait for feedback before refining.
3. Once a direction is chosen, produce the full deliverable set in that direction.
4. For each concept, briefly explain (2 sentences) the design rationale and how it ties to the "extra paws/powers" double meaning.

## Final note

The best Tabby logo is one that a developer would put on their laptop sticker, alongside their Linear, Raycast, and Ghostty stickers, without it looking out of place. Aim for that bar.
